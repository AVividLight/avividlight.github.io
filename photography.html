Street Photography

It's easy to live inside my own little bubble, content to wile away my days with an unending list of travisties and pleasures, each emerging at my very feet, and happening, most importantly, to me. Street photography helps to remind me that although we are all unique, we are united by our individuality. Every thought that I have can be matched with that of someone else, and although I stand alone in some sense, I am in good company.

Architectural Photography

Architectural photography is not considered as dynamic as streetphotography, as iconic as portraits, nor as meaningful as documentary photography, but it has a special place in my heart due to its implicit showcase of humanity. Although rarely do they feature a human itself, architectural photography is a testiment to our ingenuity, creativity, and what we can accomplish when we put aside our differences, and work together for a nobler goal.

Downtown Minneapolis

Canon EOS DIGITAL REBEL XTi
36 images, July 11, 2017

Although downtown Minneapolis doesn't feel that big, it boasts a significant history, leading back to the early industrial expansion of the United States. Emerging industries like textile production and milling relied on flowing water to power their factories, and Minneapolis proved an enticing home, being situated on the Mississippi river. Luckily, much of the character, and some of the buildings themselvs, has been preserved to this day.
